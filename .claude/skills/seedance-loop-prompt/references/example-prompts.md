## Example 1: Headphones Deconstruction (with headlines)

**Input:** "High-end headphones. Headlines: 'Engineered to disappear' / 'Give your ears a vacation'. Deconstruction reveal."

---

### SCENE
- A pair of matte black over-ear headphones with brushed aluminum hinges and perforated leather ear cushions float in a deep black void. The headphones are premium, minimal, with clean industrial lines.
- Environment: pure black void space. Fine silver particle dust drifts slowly through the background — sparse, not cluttered.
- Mood references: Apple product film meets Dyson engineering reveal. Nothing CMF teardown aesthetics.
- Color palette: near-black background, cool gunmetal silver on components, soft electric-blue accent on acoustic wave rings, warm white on text.

### CAMERA
- Single continuous 360-degree horizontal orbit at a fixed 15-degree elevation above the subject.
- Speed: slow, unhurried, cinematic. One complete rotation over the full 10-second duration.
- Starts directly facing the front of the headphones. Returns to the exact same front-facing position on the final frame.
- Distance: medium close-up — headphones fill roughly 40% of the frame height, leaving clear space above and below for text.
- Shallow depth of field with the headphones in sharp focus and particles softly blurred.

### ACTION ARC
- Starting state: headphones floating, fully assembled, at rest. No motion except ambient particle drift.
- 0-3 seconds: camera orbits slowly. Headphones remain assembled. Tension builds through stillness.
- 3-6 seconds: the headphone cups begin splitting along invisible seams — panels peel back with mechanical precision, like a flower blooming in reverse. The 40mm drivers, neodymium magnets, voice coils, acoustic dampening chambers, and mesh diaphragms float outward into orbital positions around the headphone frame. Each component glows faintly with a cool white-blue edge light.
- **5-6 seconds: PEAK VISUAL MOMENT.** All components at maximum separation. Translucent electric-blue sound wave rings pulse outward in concentric circles from the driver centers, rippling through the particle field. This is the moment of maximum visual drama.
- 6-9 seconds: components begin drifting back. Each piece finds its position with deliberate precision. Panels click shut one by one — a satisfying mechanical reconstruction.
- 9-10 seconds: headphones fully assembled, at rest, camera completing its orbit to the exact starting angle. Indistinguishable from the opening frame.

### TEXT CHOREOGRAPHY
- **"Engineered to disappear"**
  - Entrance: fades in at 2.5 seconds as the headphones begin their first hint of movement, before the full deconstruction
  - Position: upper third of frame, centered horizontally
  - Size: large — fills roughly 60% of the frame width. Dominant presence.
  - Contrast: positioned above the headphones against the pure black void. No competing elements in the upper third. Text in warm white (#F5F0E8) for soft contrast against black.
  - Exit: fades out at 5 seconds as the deconstruction reaches its peak
  - Motion: gentle fade-in over 0.8 seconds, completely static once visible, gentle fade-out over 0.5 seconds

- **"Give your ears a vacation"**
  - Entrance: fades in at 6 seconds as reconstruction begins
  - Position: lower third of frame, centered horizontally
  - Size: slightly smaller than the first headline — roughly 45% of frame width. Still clearly readable.
  - Contrast: positioned below the headphones against the pure black void. Warm white text (#F5F0E8). Clear separation from all product components.
  - Exit: fades out at 9 seconds as headphones near full reassembly
  - Motion: gentle fade-in over 0.8 seconds, static hold, gentle fade-out over 0.5 seconds

### LIGHTING & ATMOSPHERE
- Primary light: dramatic rim light from above-left at approximately 45 degrees. Cool white (5500K).
- Fill: very subtle ambient from below-right at 10% intensity — just enough to define shadow-side edges.
- Edge highlights: cool white-blue rim light catches the top edges of the headphones and floating components.
- Static lighting throughout. No flicker, no shifts, no color temperature changes at any point.
- Atmosphere: fine silver particle dust drifting slowly left to right. Sparse density — roughly 15-20 visible particles at any moment. Particles are softly out of focus.
- Particle state at frame 1 and final frame: identical density, identical distribution pattern, identical drift direction.

### LOOP SEAL
- First frame and last frame are the same reference image: headphones fully assembled, floating centered in void, camera at front-facing 0-degree position.
- Camera completes exactly one 360-degree orbit, returning to its starting position and angle.
- All deconstructed components return to their assembled positions. Headphones are fully closed and identical to the opening state.
- Particle field returns to its starting density and distribution.
- No residual motion at the loop point. The reconstruction click happens at approximately 9.5 seconds, leaving 0.5 seconds of stillness before the loop restarts.

### TECHNICAL
- Duration: 10 seconds
- Seamlessly looping video
- Image-to-video generation mode: use the same image for first frame and last frame
- No watermarks
- 4K resolution

> **Seedance setup:** Use image-to-video mode. Set the same image as both the first frame and last frame reference to ensure a seamless loop.

## Example 2: Luxury Watch (background plate only, no text)

**Input:** "Luxury dive watch, dark and moody."

---

- Mood references: Omega Seamaster campaign films. TAG Heuer "Don't Crack Under Pressure" cinematics.
- Color palette: near-black background, cold steel silver, deep navy-black on the dial, faint aquamarine on caustic light refractions.

### CAMERA
- Single continuous 360-degree horizontal orbit at a fixed 10-degree elevation.
- Speed: very slow, deliberate, weighty. One full rotation over 10 seconds.
- Starts at the 9 o'clock side of the watch (profile view showing crown). Returns to the exact same profile position.
- Distance: tight medium shot — watch fills approximately 50% of frame height.
- Moderate depth of field — watch face sharp, bracelet links gradually soften toward the edges.

### ACTION ARC
- Starting state: watch floating, fully assembled, crown-side profile visible. Minute hand at the 12 position.
- 0-3 seconds: camera orbits, revealing the face. The watch is still, solid, inert. The caustic water light drifts slowly across the void below.
- 3-5 seconds: the bezel begins to lift and rotate slowly, hovering 2cm above the case. The sapphire crystal rises next, tilting slightly to catch a rim-light flash. The dial lifts, revealing the automatic movement underneath — gears, balance wheel, mainspring barrel.
- **5-7 seconds: PEAK VISUAL MOMENT.** The movement is fully exposed. The balance wheel oscillates in slow motion. Gears turn with mechanical precision. Every component is separated and floating in a vertical stack, each piece rotating independently at slightly different speeds. The caustic light below intensifies slightly.

- 9-10 seconds: watch fully assembled, camera completing orbit to the exact starting profile angle. Identical to frame 1.

### TEXT CHOREOGRAPHY
No text. No UI. Background plate only.

### LIGHTING & ATMOSPHERE

- Edge highlights: thin white rim light on the case edges and crown. Steel catches light as the camera orbits, creating a slow-moving specular sweep.
- Static lighting throughout. No shifts, no flicker, no changes. The specular highlights move only because the camera orbits, not because the light moves.
- Atmosphere: faint caustic light patterns on the lower background — slow-moving, aquamarine-tinted, as if light is refracting through gently moving water far below. Very subtle. Never competes with the watch.
- Caustic pattern at frame 1 and final frame: identical position, identical intensity.

### LOOP SEAL

- Camera completes exactly one 360-degree orbit, returning to its starting position.
- All deconstructed components return to their assembled positions. Watch is fully sealed.

## Example 3: Sports Car City Flythrough (with one headline)

**Input:** "Electric sports car. Headline: 'Silent fury'. Night city."

---

### SCENE
- A matte midnight-blue electric sports car with sharp angular lines, LED light blade running across the full rear width, and flush door handles. Low, wide stance.
- Environment: a rain-slicked urban boulevard at night. Neon signage reflects in the wet asphalt — pink, cyan, warm amber. Tall glass buildings on both sides create a canyon effect. Light fog at street level.
- Mood references: Porsche Taycan launch film. Blade Runner 2049 cityscape sequences. Tron Legacy light cycle aesthetics.
- Color palette: deep midnight blue (car), black asphalt, neon pink and cyan reflections, warm amber street lights, cool white LED accents.

### CAMERA
- Low-angle tracking shot, camera positioned approximately 30cm above the road surface, 3 meters behind and slightly left of the car.
- The car drives forward along the boulevard. Camera maintains fixed distance and angle throughout.
- Speed: the car accelerates gently from near-still to moderate cruising speed, then decelerates back to near-still.
- Start position = end position: the car is in the same position relative to the camera, at the same speed (near-still), in an identical stretch of boulevard.
- The city environment is designed to tile — the building facades and neon signs repeat seamlessly so the loop point is invisible.

### ACTION ARC
- Starting state: car near-still, centered in the boulevard. LED light blade glowing steadily. Neon reflections shimmering on wet asphalt.
- 0-3 seconds: car begins to accelerate. The wet road streaks with elongated neon reflections as speed increases. Light fog parts gently around the car's body. LED brake lights cast a soft pink glow on the road behind.
- 3-5 seconds: moderate cruising speed. The camera holds steady. Neon signs pass overhead. Rain-slicked reflections streak in long lines beneath the car.
- **5-7 seconds: PEAK VISUAL MOMENT.** The car passes directly under a large neon sign — cyan and pink light washes across the car's entire body in a slow sweep, revealing every contour and surface line. For 1.5 seconds, the car is fully illuminated in colored light. The wet road beneath becomes a mirror of neon.
- 7-9 seconds: car begins to decelerate. Neon reflections shorten. Fog closes back in gently. The boulevard returns to the same visual density as the opening.
- 9-10 seconds: car near-still again. Camera at the same relative position and angle. Environment identical to the opening — same neon density, same fog, same reflection patterns.

### TEXT CHOREOGRAPHY
- **"Silent fury"**
  - Entrance: appears at 4 seconds as the car reaches cruising speed
  - Position: upper-left third of frame. Left-aligned. Sits in the dark sky area above the building line where there is no visual competition.
  - Size: large — text spans roughly 35% of frame width. Bold, condensed sans-serif feel. All uppercase.
  - Contrast: white text against the dark night sky above the buildings. The upper portion of the frame is consistently dark (no neon reaches that high), ensuring the text is always readable. No additional gradient needed — the natural darkness of the sky provides contrast.
  - Exit: fades out at 7.5 seconds as the car decelerates past the neon peak
  - Motion: slides in from the left edge over 0.6 seconds, holds, then fades out over 0.4 seconds

### LIGHTING & ATMOSPHERE
- No single key light — the scene is lit entirely by practical sources: neon signs, street lights, the car's own LEDs.
- Neon sources: pink, cyan, and warm amber at varying heights along the boulevard. They create colored light pools on the wet road.
- Static lighting throughout: the neon signs do not flicker, blink, or change color. They are constant, steady sources. Only the car's position relative to them changes.
- Atmospheric fog: thin ground-level fog, consistent density throughout. Does not shift, thicken, or clear.
- Rain: the road is wet but it is not actively raining. No raindrops visible. Just reflective wet surfaces.
- Environmental state at frame 1 and final frame: identical fog density, identical neon positions, identical reflection patterns on the road.

### LOOP SEAL
- First frame and last frame are the same reference image: car near-still in the boulevard, low camera angle, neon reflections on wet road.
- Car returns to the same speed (near-still) and same position relative to camera.
- Fog density, neon reflection patterns, and ambient light levels identical at start and end.
- The boulevard environment is designed to feel continuous — no landmark buildings or unique signs that would reveal the loop point.
- 0.3 seconds of near-stillness at both the start and end to create an invisible seam.

### TECHNICAL
- Duration: 10 seconds
- Seamlessly looping video
- Image-to-video generation mode: use the same image for first frame and last frame
- No watermarks
- 4K resolution

> **Seedance setup:** Use image-to-video mode. Set the same image as both the first frame and last frame reference to ensure a seamless loop.
